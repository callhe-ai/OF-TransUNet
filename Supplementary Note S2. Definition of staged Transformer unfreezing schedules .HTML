Supplementary Note Sx. Definition of staged Transformer unfreezing schedules

To improve implementation transparency, we summarize here how the staged unfreezing schedules were defined in the internal development experiments.

No Fine-tuning

The inserted Conv-Transformer remained frozen throughout training, and only the nnU-Net backbone and decoder were updated.

Full Fine-tuning

All Transformer parameter groups were trainable from the beginning of training, without any frozen stabilization phase.

Simple Progressive

In the Simple Progressive schedule, the inserted Transformer was partitioned into four predefined parameter groups in the implementation and released sequentially in the default order [1, 2, 3, 4]. The Transformer remained frozen for epochs 0–69, after which one parameter group was released every 20 epochs, corresponding to release onsets at epochs 70, 90, 110, and 130.

Output-Focused (final)

In the final implemented OF-TransUNet schedule, the Transformer remained frozen through epochs 0–89. At epoch 90, output-side Transformer components began to be released, and the optimizer was reconfigured to include the newly trainable parameters. A short Transformer-specific warmup was then applied during epochs 90–94, increasing the Transformer branch learning rate from 0.000090 to 0.000450.

Additional optimizer reconfiguration events were observed later in training as newly trainable Transformer parameters were incorporated. However, the preserved archived logs consistently documented output-side release events rather than a fully enumerated mapping of later releases to individual internal Transformer sublayers or block-depth groups. Accordingly, the final OF-TransUNet schedule is described as an empirically implemented output-focused staged adaptation strategy rather than as a strictly layer-wise protocol.

Interpretive note

The output-focused schedule should therefore be understood as prioritizing output-proximal Transformer components during staged release, with clearly documented epoch boundaries for the initial frozen phase and warmup phase, but without over-interpreting later internal subdivision beyond what was retained in the archived training logs.
